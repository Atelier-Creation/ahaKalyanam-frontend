The Method of Searching for a Match

This message is to guide you on the right path to marriage. For a prospective match to get married soon, they must meet the age, qualification, and citizenship requirements as per the Hindu Marriage Act of India. Furthermore, the man and woman must have attained the minimum marital age with sufficient physical and mental maturity to run a family.

1. Prepare Your Biodata

It is customary to register with matrimonial bureaus and websites to get married soon. You must prepare a one-page biodata that includes: date of birth, education, job/income, address, and two mobile numbers. Get this professionally typed (DTP) and always keep at least ten copies, along with ten good-quality, postcard-sized photos. Using an old, tiny photo is the biggest reason for marriage delays!

2. Spend Wisely

Increase your registrations with various information centers—the cost is low, but the rewards are high. Avoid marriage organizers or commission brokers who do not provide complete information, as they lead to wasted effort and expenses. Registering with community-based matrimonial centers is highly beneficial.

3. Choose the Right Platform

It is best to register on matrimonial websites that do not trouble you by repeatedly asking for more money. You can send a copy of your complete horoscope details with a photograph to our Thillai Matrimonial Center, and we will register it for free.

4. The Search Process

A positive mindset and experience increase the chances of a quick marriage. Write down all your expectations (age, education, height, family, etc.) in a notebook. Contact prospects over the phone and speak directly with the family. If both parties are interested, exchange biodata and photos via email or post.

5. Involve Families

Arrange to meet a prospective match only with the consent of elders from both families to avoid mental distress and financial waste. Remember there are all kinds of people in the world; continue your efforts with confidence.

6. Understanding Responses

If someone hesitates or postpones giving a clear "yes" or "no," it likely means they are not interested. You can inquire about the reason if you wish.
